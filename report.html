FILE: ST12-ICD/ABT-096

INCIDENT: ABOTILLA EVENT

A containment breach involving SCP-096 originated at Site-██, a mid-tier secure holding installation operated by the SCP Foundation. The site was not considered high-risk; SCP-096, while lethal under specific conditions, was classified as manageable under strict visual control protocols. The containment chamber was structurally sound, isolated, and monitored. The failure did not arise from the object. It arose from procedure.

ORIGIN OF BREACH

On ██/██/20██, during a scheduled maintenance and inspection cycle, a junior researcher—designation withheld—was assigned to assess surface degradation within SCP-096’s containment cell. Standard protocol required the use of non-visual instrumentation: LiDAR scanning, depth mapping, and indirect sensor arrays. Direct imaging was explicitly prohibited.

At 03:12, the researcher deviated from protocol. A handheld optical device was used to capture a still image of the chamber interior. The researcher’s report later indicated that SCP-096 was positioned facing the far wall and that no facial exposure occurred during direct observation. This assessment was incorrect.

The captured image contained a partial reflection of SCP-096’s face on a metallic surface within the chamber. The visible portion was incomplete—estimated at less than 20% of total facial area—but sufficient. The researcher did not recognize the error.

At 03:13, the image was uploaded into Site-██’s internal documentation system. From there, it entered automated processes: archival backup, redundancy mirroring, and cross-site synchronization. A misconfigured network bridge transmitted the file beyond Foundation-controlled infrastructure. At 03:14, the image exited containment.

INITIAL PROPAGATION

The first confirmed external access occurred in the rural settlement of Abotilla, approximately 18 kilometers from Site-██. A civilian contractor accessed the corrupted data node while performing routine system maintenance. The image was downloaded automatically and opened momentarily. That single exposure activated SCP-096.

Due to cloud synchronization, the image replicated across multiple local devices—personal phones, shared terminals, and backup systems. Within approximately two minutes, at least 30 individuals in Abotilla had viewed the image, either directly or incidentally. No one in the village understood the significance.

ACTIVATION AND TRANSIT

At 03:20, SCP-096 entered an active state. Foundation monitoring systems registered the behavioral shift almost immediately. SCP-096 began direct traversal toward Abotilla, moving uninterrupted across terrain and ignoring obstacles. Estimated transit time: 8–9 minutes.

Exposure within the village continued to increase as individuals shared devices, attempted to interpret the image, or inadvertently viewed it via reflections. Panic had not yet begun. The event had not yet manifested physically.

THE ABOTILLA INCIDENT

At approximately 03:29, SCP-096 entered the village perimeter. There was no warning. The first recorded damage occurred at the northeastern residential cluster. Structural analysis indicates direct-force entry through external walls, followed by immediate removal of occupants.

Phase I — Confusion (03:29–03:32)

Residents reported loud impacts, collapsing structures, and isolated screams. No clear threat was identified. Some individuals attempted to investigate.

Phase II — Recognition Failure (03:32–03:36)

Multiple casualties occurred in rapid sequence. Survivors began to flee or barricade themselves, though without understanding the nature of the threat. Several individuals attempted to contact emergency services or record the event. These actions increased exposure.

Phase III — Cascade (03:36–03:42)

The situation escalated into full systemic collapse. Vehicles collided during attempted evacuation. Fires ignited due to ruptured gas lines and electrical faults. Civilians continued to interact with devices containing the image. SCP-096’s activity intensified proportionally to the number of active targets.

Phase IV — Terminal Sweep (03:42–04:11)

Nearly all individuals who had viewed the image had been located. SCP-096’s movements became more isolated, targeting remaining individuals in outlying structures and concealed positions. One confirmed case involved an individual hiding in a windowless storage area who had viewed only a fragmented reflection of the image.

FOUNDATION RESPONSE

At 03:44, Sector Twelve identified the event as an information-origin cascade involving SCP-096. Immediate suppression protocols were initiated. Mobile Task Force Eta-10 (“See No Evil”) was deployed. Units established a perimeter without entering the active zone.

Using non-optical systems—thermal imaging, acoustic sensors, and LiDAR mapping—they tracked SCP-096’s position and movement. Intervention was deliberately withheld. SCP-096 cannot be neutralized during an active cycle; attempting to do so would introduce additional exposure risk.

At 04:11, SCP-096 terminated the final exposed individual within a central structure. The entity entered a dormant state. Containment was re-established without direct visual contact. SCP-096 was secured and transported back to Site-██ under reinforced protocol.

SURVIVORS

Nineteen individuals survived. All had not seen the image. Survival was circumstantial; some were asleep, absent, or shielded from devices. Psychological assessments identified acute stress disorder, auditory hallucinations, and intrusive mental constructs resembling the entity.

Amnestic treatment was administered. Fourteen subjects responded as expected, five demonstrated partial resistance. All survivors were relocated under new identities.

ERASURE OF ABOTILLA

By 36 hours post-incident, Abotilla was effectively erased. Remaining structures were demolished under controlled conditions. Digital records were altered or removed, geographic references minimized in public databases.

PUBLIC STATEMENT

The narrative disseminated through regional authorities claimed a gas pipeline rupture and subsequent fires. Casualties were reported as standard emergency incidents. Public acceptance exceeded 90%.

CONCLUSION

The Abotilla Incident was redefined. The failure did not occur when SCP-096 left its chamber; it occurred when its image left the system. Sector Twelve’s final assessment:

“In the modern operational environment, containment is no longer spatial. It is informational. The entity did not escape. The data did.”
