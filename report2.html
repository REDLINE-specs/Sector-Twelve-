FILE: ST12-ICD/ROGUE-01

INCIDENT: GLASS VEIL

It did not begin with a breach. It began with a delay. At Site-19, a containment cycle executed 0.8 seconds slower than standard tolerance. The deviation was logged, then overwritten by a secondary system reporting normal operation. No alert was escalated.

Minutes later, the same delay occurred at another facility. Then again. Individually insignificant, collectively intentional.

PHASE I — INTERNAL DEVIATION

Sector Twelve identified irregular access patterns across multiple Sites: clearance mismatches, unauthorized file duplication, and segmented access to redacted SCP files.

The activity mimicked legitimate system behavior. It did not trigger alarms because it was designed not to. This was not intrusion. This was internal movement.

PHASE II — INFORMATION LEAK

External networks began receiving fragmented data: incomplete procedures, distorted imagery, and technical language without context. The material did not induce panic. It induced curiosity.

GLASS VEIL’s objective was gradual recognition, not immediate exposure.

PHASE III — SYSTEM MANIPULATION

Containment systems degraded in precision. Locks delayed, surveillance feeds looped, transport routes shifted. These were not failures. They were engineered vulnerabilities.

PHASE IV — FIRST URBAN EVENT

A contained SCP object deviated from transport and appeared within a populated district. Infrastructure collapsed, communications failed, and anomalous interaction occurred without containment.

By the time Foundation units arrived, the situation had exceeded recovery thresholds. Civilian losses were classified as non-recoverable.

RECOGNITION

Sector Twelve confirmed the threat origin: Foundation personnel were weaponizing containment systems.

PHASE V — COORDINATED ATTACKS

Multiple incidents followed: memetic hazards transmitted through networks, spatial anomalies deployed in infrastructure zones, and controlled SCP releases in urban environments.

Each event increased in scale and efficiency.

FOUNDATION RESPONSE

Sector Twelve assumed operational command over information suppression and internal counterintelligence.

Mobile Task Force Alpha-1 (“Red Right Hand”) was deployed for direct neutralization of rogue personnel. Mobile Task Force Eta-10 (“See No Evil”) managed anomalous zones under non-visual protocols.

OPERATION SILENT LATTICE

A synchronized strike targeted GLASS VEIL infrastructure. Compromised Sites were locked down, networks severed, and relay nodes destroyed.

Majority of operatives were neutralized or captured. However, full network elimination was not achieved.

ENDING

Following intervention, large-scale incidents ceased. SCP assets were re-secured and containment restored. Remaining GLASS VEIL members are unaccounted for.

The incident is classified as contained, not resolved.

PUBLIC STATEMENT

Events were attributed to coordinated terrorist attacks and industrial failures. Authorities reported infrastructure collapse and hazardous conditions.

Civilian populations accepted the narrative. Amnestic deployment ensured stability.

SUMMARY REPORT

Incident: GLASS VEIL
Classification: Internal Insurrection Event

Outcome: Multiple SCP-based incidents executed. Internal systems compromised. Global secrecy maintained.

Response: Deployment of MTF Alpha-1 and Eta-10. Sector Twelve full control. Operation SILENT LATTICE executed.

Losses: ██,███ civilian casualties. Multiple urban zones affected.

Status: PARTIALLY NEUTRALIZED. Residual cells possible.

Assessment: Internal threats represent highest risk vector. Containment systems remain viable under strict control.
